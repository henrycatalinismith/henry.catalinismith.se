Recently I did this little panel discussion at Unionen HQ about union organising
in the Swedish tech industry. It was great fun. We talked for ages and everyone
said lots of cool stuff. I think this was the most important thing I said.

> There is a sort of helplessness… like a learned helplessness. There's this
> idea that okay, these other workers in this industry that industry and that
> industry they all got like union deals, collective agreements at their jobs
> through conflict and so on back in the 20th century. But that was then. This
> is now. That was those industries. We can't do that because all we know about
> is computers and that was all a long time ago. It bothers me this idea that
> “oh _we_ can't do it, _we_ can't have that”.
>
> Yes we can. You can just do it. You just start. Just start. That's all. As
> long as you start, you can have it. The trick is just to not let anyone talk
> you out of starting that's all. Because there's a lot of voices out there who
> would like to talk you out of starting. They'll tell you that's not for us, we
> can't have that.
>
> Don't let them tell you what you can achieve, you know. You can have this.

I also fell in love with the accidental comedy of this one frame from the video.

![still from video with a sad face and the caption "all we know about is coputers" superimposed](/2024/11/06/all-we-know-about-is-computers@256x256.webp)

Captures perfectly how people are intended to feel when their anti-union
employer tells them
[MBL § 11](https://lagstiftning.codeberg.page/codetermination-act/2021:1114/section-11)
negotiations would slow things down too much. Unionen's
[frihet.unionen.se](https://www.frihet.unionen.se/en) campaign is worth a look
if that sounds like your workplace BTW.
